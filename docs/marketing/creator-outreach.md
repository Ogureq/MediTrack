# Gemocode Creator Outreach Kit

Supports Engine A in `docs/MARKETING.md` (creator-led short video, the Cal AI
playbook adapted to chronic-illness/nurse-educator/longevity niches). Lead
message for every outreach touch: never lose a lab result, know exactly when
your next one is due — and when it isn't — and walk into any appointment
with your full history so nothing gets re-ordered from scratch. Privacy is
the strong #2, always present, never leading. Every template below assumes
the same compliance floor as the rest of `docs/marketing/`: no medical
claims, educational framing only, FTC disclosure on every paid post, no
overpromising (iOS-only, pre-launch — adjust the "download" language to
"waitlist" until launch day actually happens), and accurate pricing —
tracking (including the Tests Due retest schedule) is free forever; lab
scanning and every AI feature (report narration, chat, AI-assisted entry)
are Premium at $19.99/mo, with one free lifetime scan-and-report as a trial.
Never describe scanning, OCR, or AI-assisted entry as free.

## 3. Negotiation cheat-sheet — rate bands by follower count

All figures are flat-fee starting points *(est.)*, not fixed prices — first
offer, expect to land within the band after a short back-and-forth.
Anchored to the ≤$5 CPM renewal bar from `docs/MARKETING.md`: if a
creator's typical views would blow past $5 CPM at the offered flat fee,
offer nearer the bottom of the band; if their views run low for their
follower count, offer nearer the bottom and rely on the referral code to
make up the difference rather than raising the flat fee.

| Follower band | Flat fee per video *(est.)* | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 5,000–20,000 | $50–150 | Micro tier — the volume play. Most of the outreach budget goes here; expect variable view counts, judge renewal by actual CPM after video 1, not follower count. |
| 20,000–50,000 | $150–400 | Mid tier — ask for a rough average-views number before naming a figure so the offer lands near the $5 CPM line, not over it. |
| 50,000–100,000 | $400–800 | Upper tier — worth a short call before committing; verify recent view counts (screenshot or public analytics) rather than relying on follower count, which is a weak proxy at this size. |

Standing rules regardless of band:
- Referral code is always in addition to the flat fee, never instead of it.
- No exclusivity asks at any tier for the first video — exclusivity is a
  later-stage conversation once a creator is proven.
- Payment on delivery/posting, not before — but confirm the video's content
  passes the creator brief's "never say" list *before* it posts, not after.
- Renewal (a second, third, ... video) is contingent on the previous video
  clearing roughly a $5 CPM (spend ÷ views × 1000). Below that twice in a
  row, pause the relationship rather than renegotiating a lower fee — per
  `docs/MARKETING.md`'s "drop creators who miss 2× in a row" rule.
- Rates in this table are starting points for a pre-launch, zero-revenue,
  solo-founder budget; revisit them once real CPM data exists.

## 4. Creator brief — what to send once a creator says yes

### What to show
- **The Tests Due moment, front and center.** The dashboard card lighting up
  "overdue" on one test and "not due yet — skip it" on another, computed
  from the creator's own (or a realistic sample) test history. This is the
  freshest, most demoable moment in the app — lead with it, don't bury it
  at the end.
- **The scan demo, as a strong second beat.** Photographing a real (or
  realistic sample) lab report, Gemocode reading the values on-device, and
  the status-pill result appearing. Note in the brief that this is a
  Premium feature with one free scan to try — don't caption it "free."
- Their own authentic framing of *why* this mattered to them (a real lab
  result, a real frustration with re-Googling markers, a real duplicate-test
  story) — don't hand them a script to read verbatim; give them the facts
  and let them say it in their own voice.
- Optional secondary beats if the format allows: the no-login cold open
  ("there's no sign-up screen") or the quarterly review recap — pick one,
  don't cram all of them in. (The Quick Add sentence-to-record moment is
  fine to show too — typed shorthand like "bp 128/82" parses on-device and
  is free; only the AI button for whole multi-item sentences is Premium.
  Caption whichever variant is actually shown.)
- The free/paid split stated plainly: tracking — including the Tests Due
  retest schedule, health score, trends, and Quarterly Review — is free
  forever. Lab scanning and every AI feature (report narration, chat,
  AI-assisted entry) are Premium at $19.99/mo, with one free lifetime
  scan-and-report as a trial.
- Retest intervals shown on-screen are always captioned "commonly
  recommended — your doctor may advise differently," never stated as a fixed
  rule.
- The #ad/#sponsored disclosure, per platform requirements, visible in the
  actual post (caption and/or spoken in the first few seconds) — not just
  buried in a description field nobody reads.

### What to never say (hard list — zero exceptions)
Do not use, in any form, spoken or on-screen text:
- "Diagnose," "diagnosis," or any claim the app diagnoses anything.
- "Treats," "cures," "prevents," or "improves" a disease/condition.
- "Detects disease," "catches [condition] early," or similar detection claims.
- Any claim of clinical accuracy, lab-grade precision, or medical-device status.
- Any implication the app or its AI is a doctor, replaces a doctor, or should be trusted over a doctor.
- Promises of health outcomes ("you'll feel better," "your numbers will improve").
- Comparative health-outcome claims against named competitors or against not using any tracker.
- Overstated privacy claims beyond what's true (e.g., "100% private" when AI is opted into — the accurate line is "no account, on-device storage, AI is opt-in and only sends a summary").
- Any claim the app is available on Android, web, or before its actual iOS launch date.
- "Revolutionary," "game-changing," or other hype language — keep it plain and specific, same voice as the rest of the brand's content.

If a creator's draft caption/script contains any of the above, ask for a
revision before it posts — this is worth a short delay, every time.

### Reference materials to send with the brief
- A demo TestFlight build or screen-recording clip of the Tests Due card and
  the scan flow.
- 2–3 of the 30-second scripts from `docs/marketing/content-bank.md` as
  optional inspiration (not a mandatory script).
- The one-sentence positioning line: "Know when you're due. Know when
  you're not." (with the follow-up line, always kept close behind: "No
  account. Nothing leaves your phone.")
